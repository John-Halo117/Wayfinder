| Reality | What exists or occurred before representation. | Anchor truth and evidence. | Constitution. | Observed by Perception; preserved through ARK. | Reality precedes all models. | Cannot be redefined by derived systems. |

| Representation | A description, projection, encoding, index, or view of reality. | Enable use without replacing sources. | Architecture. | Derived from ARK-preserved reality. | Rebuildable and subordinate to observations. | Can be regenerated or deleted if sources remain. |

| Contract | Stable boundary language or schema. | Let implementations interoperate safely. | `contracts/`. | Used by services, engines, domains. | Contracts cannot depend on implementations. | Change through versioned compatibility. |
| Service | Reusable infrastructure. | Provide shared capabilities without engine duplication. | `services/`. | Consumed by engines and domains. | Services must not depend on engines. | Extract only after proof of reuse. |
| Engine | Unique responsibility implementing a capability workflow. | Own domain-independent specialized behavior. | `engines/`. | Uses services and contracts; feeds domains/apps. | Engines do not own shared infrastructure. | Legacy engines migrate by proof. |
| Domain | Orchestration around a real workflow or mission. | Compose capabilities for situated use. | `domains/`. | Depends on contracts, services, engines. | Domains do not redefine lower concepts. | Add only when real workflow exists. |
| Internal Application | User or operator surface. | Present and operate Wayfinder capabilities. | `internal/`. | Consumes domains/services/engines through contracts. | Presentation does not own truth. | UI can evolve independently of preservation. |
| External Integration | Replaceable outside provider boundary. | Connect external systems without provider lock-in. | `external/`. | Feeds perception or actions through compatibility. | Provider code remains replaceable. | Compatibility layers preserve legacy names while canonical ownership matures. |
| Operation | Deployment, recovery, observability, migration, maintenance. | Keep Wayfinder runable and recoverable. | `operations/`. | Acts on deploy targets and runtime health. | Operations do not redefine architecture. | Operational maturity advances explicitly. |
