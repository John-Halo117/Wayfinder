# CivPhys

CivPhys is Wayfinder's constitutional mechanics layer. It describes how systems behave before domain-specific interpretation is introduced.

CivPhys is universal. A homestead, family, organization, software system, financial structure, infrastructure system, ecosystem, government, or continuity basin should be expressible in CivPhys before specialized abstractions are added.

## Purpose

CivPhys provides a small mechanics vocabulary for reasoning about systems across domains.

It exists to keep Wayfinder from multiplying domain-specific concepts when a universal mechanical description is sufficient.

## Relationship To Reality

Reality is what is observed and preserved.

CivPhys is the mechanics vocabulary used to describe patterns in reality. CivPhys does not replace observations, evidence, or provenance. It gives interpretation and reasoning a stable way to describe potential, pressure, flow, and boundaries observed in reality.

## Relationship To Capabilities

Capabilities describe stable outcomes Wayfinder can pursue.

CivPhys describes the mechanics that make capabilities possible, constrained, blocked, amplified, or transformed. A capability may unlock potential, redirect flow, alter pressure, or change a membrane.

## Relationship To Navigation

Navigation chooses bearings and routes under uncertainty.

CivPhys supplies navigation with mechanical structure: available potential, active pressure, viable flows, and membranes that permit, constrain, or transform movement. Jarvis uses this structure but does not own CivPhys.

## Irreducible Primitives

CivPhys has exactly four irreducible primitives.

### Potential

Potential is stored capability.

It represents latent possibility that could become real under the right conditions. Potential may be capacity, knowledge, trust, energy, material, optionality, authority, capital, attention, or any other stored ability to produce future change.

Potential is not flow. A system can hold potential without currently moving it.

### Pressure

Pressure is demand, incentive, constraint, risk, or force acting on a system.

Pressure makes potential relevant, stresses membranes, redirects flows, and creates urgency. Pressure may come from needs, hazards, obligations, scarcity, goals, conflict, time-sensitive conditions, or external forces.

Pressure is not potential. Pressure may exist even when the system lacks capacity to respond.

### Flow

Flow is movement.

Flow may be movement of resources, information, energy, people, attention, capability, obligations, authority, or other conserved quantities. Flow is how systems change state and exchange with other systems.

Flow is not potential. Flow is active movement, not stored possibility.

### Membrane

A membrane is a boundary that regulates, constrains, permits, filters, or transforms flows.

Membranes include physical barriers, roles, permissions, contracts, interfaces, institutions, trust boundaries, governance, rituals, habits, procedures, and identity boundaries.

A membrane is not merely pressure. A membrane may regulate flow even when no immediate demand is acting on it.

## Time

Time is not a CivPhys primitive.

Time is the dimension over which Potential, Pressure, Flow, and Membrane evolve. Mechanics such as resilience, entropy, fragility, evolution, and feedback require time, but time does not add a fifth kind of mechanical thing.

## Canonical Derivation Rules

- Do not introduce a new primitive if a concept can be derived from Potential, Pressure, Flow, Membrane, and Time.
- Derived mechanics must name the primitive roots they emerge from.
- Domain examples do not create domain-specific primitives.
- A derived mechanic may be important without being fundamental.
- A derived mechanic may become canonical language without becoming a primitive.
- If a concept requires evidence, identity, policy, storage, or navigation ownership, classify it outside CivPhys.

## Rules For Extending CivPhys

A proposed CivPhys addition must answer yes to all of the following:

1. Is it domain-independent?
2. Is it mechanical rather than semantic, operational, or implementation-specific?
3. Can it not be derived from the four primitives and time?
4. Would every Wayfinder subsystem benefit from it?
5. Does it reduce rather than increase long-term ambiguity?

If any answer is no, the concept remains derived or belongs elsewhere.

## Ownership

CivPhys belongs in `constitution/`.

Engines may use CivPhys, but no engine owns CivPhys. Domains may specialize CivPhys examples, but domains do not create new CivPhys primitives.
