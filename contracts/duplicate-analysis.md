# Duplicate Contract Analysis

| Potential Duplicate | Decision | Canonical Resolution |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Asset vs Representation | Not duplicate | Asset identity is distinct from descriptions, views, metadata, summaries, and projections. |
| Evidence vs Proof | Not duplicate | Evidence supports claims; Proof evaluates whether evidence satisfies promotion criteria. |
| Bearing vs Recommendation | Not duplicate | Bearing orients; Recommendation proposes a route or action. |
| Recommendation vs Commitment | Not duplicate | Recommendation advises; Commitment records accountability and acceptance. |
| Relationship vs Context | Not duplicate | Context frames interpretation; Relationship connects assets. |
| Capability vs Transformation | Not duplicate | Capability names outcome potential; Transformation maps change paths. |
| Capsule vs Specification | Not duplicate | Capsule preserves re-entry continuity; Specification preserves canonical requirements. |
| Promotion vs Proof | Not duplicate | Proof justifies; Promotion makes durable in a canonical owner. |

## Rule

When two contracts appear to overlap, keep the contract whose producer owns the constitutional responsibility and reference the other contract rather than duplicating its language.
