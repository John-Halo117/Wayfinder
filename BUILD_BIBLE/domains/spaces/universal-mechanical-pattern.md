# Universal Mechanical Pattern

Mechanical spaces, utility chases, service corridors, and equipment zones are
physical infrastructure for future change.

## Mechanical Rooms

Mechanical rooms should provide:

- equipment identity and clearances
- replacement path
- isolation points
- drain or spill strategy
- ventilation
- lighting
- working receptacles
- documentation location
- monitoring
- safe service access

## Utility Chases

Utility chases should provide:

- known route
- access points
- separation rules
- fire, moisture, pest, and acoustic constraints
- spare path capacity
- pull or replacement method
- route evidence

## Service Corridors

Service corridors should provide:

- human access to critical infrastructure
- lighting
- safe walking or working surface
- labeled service zones
- emergency egress constraints
- expansion route capacity

## Equipment Clearances

Clearances are reserved physical capacity. They must not be consumed by storage
unless the affected maintenance and replacement paths are updated.

## Future Equipment

Mechanical spaces should reserve floor area, wall area, panel capacity, drain
capacity, ventilation capacity, and data paths for future equipment.

## Replacement Paths

Major equipment must have a path from installed location to exterior or service
vehicle route without destructive removal of permanent assemblies when
practical.
