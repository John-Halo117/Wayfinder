# Universal Wet Area Pattern

Wet areas include bathrooms, laundry rooms, mudrooms, greenhouses, mechanical
rooms, wash bays, utility rooms, and any space where water is normal.

## Required Capabilities

- water-resistant or waterproof assemblies appropriate to exposure
- drainage strategy
- ventilation
- leak detection strategy where practical
- serviceable water interfaces
- serviceable drain interfaces
- cleanable surfaces
- access to shutoffs
- documented failure behavior

## Drainage

Wet areas should define floor slope, floor drains where appropriate, fixture
drains, cleanouts, overflow behavior, and downstream consequences.

## Waterproofing

Waterproofing must identify protected surfaces, seams, penetrations, edges,
curbs, transitions, inspection limits, and expected service life.

## Access Panels

Valves, traps, pumps, manifolds, sensors, filters, and drain cleanouts should
remain accessible through panels, cabinets, adjacent service zones, or exposed
utility walls.

## Utility Walls

Wet areas should concentrate water, drainage, ventilation, power, and controls
in utility walls or service zones when practical.

## Sensors

Wet areas should reserve locations for leak, humidity, temperature, occupancy,
flow, drain, and equipment-status sensors where the risk justifies monitoring.

## Ventilation

Ventilation must document source, route, discharge, controls, access, and
failure behavior.

## Maintenance

Maintenance includes testing shutoffs, inspecting seals, cleaning drains,
checking ventilation, verifying sensors, and recording moisture observations.

## Future Expansion

Wet areas should preserve spare water, drain, power, data, ventilation, and
mounting capacity for future fixtures and equipment.
