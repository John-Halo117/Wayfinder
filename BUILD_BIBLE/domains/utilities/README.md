# Utilities Domain

Owns shared utility doctrine and cross-utility coordination.

Canonical here:

- utility corridor coordination
- shared chase rules
- utility separation rules
- utility labeling conventions
- cross-domain serviceability requirements

Specific utility systems are owned by their narrower domains.

## Purpose

Coordinate cross-utility standards so power, water, waste, HVAC,
communications, lighting, monitoring, security, and automation remain explicit,
serviceable, and replaceable.

- [Utility Standards](standards/README.md)

## Relationships

Utility standards depend on [Interface Contract](../../contracts/interface-contract.md),
[Capability Contract](../../contracts/capability-contract.md), and
[Capacity Contract](../../contracts/capacity-contract.md).

## Lifecycle

Utility standards are reusable. Installed utility systems are instantiated in
the narrowest owning domain with observations and service records.

## Generation Targets

Utility maps, one-line diagrams, valve maps, panel schedules, rack elevations,
control diagrams, and isolation guides.
