# Property Pattern

A property is the root physical platform for land, access, buildings,
utilities, water movement, agriculture, security, maintenance, and future
expansion.

## Land Philosophy

Land is not blank space. It is an active system of slopes, soils, water,
microclimates, access paths, living systems, legal constraints, and future
options.

Design begins by preserving what the land already does, then improving
serviceability and resilience without erasing future choices.

## Site Analysis

Every property instance should document:

- legal boundary and easements
- topography and slope
- soil behavior
- sun, shade, wind, frost, and heat exposure
- surface water movement
- existing vegetation and habitat
- access and emergency approach
- utility availability and ingress constraints
- buildable, serviceable, protected, and reserved zones
- hazards, setbacks, and governing restrictions

## Utility Ingress

Utility ingress should converge through a legible property spine with:

- known entry points
- isolation points
- metering or monitoring points
- accessible transitions
- route evidence
- reserved parallel paths
- clear separation between incompatible services

## Roads And Access

Roads, drives, paths, gates, loading zones, and service pads should preserve:

- emergency access
- material delivery
- equipment movement
- maintenance circulation
- pedestrian safety
- drainage behavior
- future construction access

## Drainage And Water Movement

Water movement is a first-class site system. The property pattern should define
normal flow, overflow, storage, infiltration, erosion risk, and inspection
points before placing buildings or roads.

## Future Expansion

Property records should reserve:

- utility corridors
- future building sites
- renewable energy zones
- water storage zones
- agricultural expansion zones
- communications and monitoring locations
- unknown future technology zones

## Observation Points

Observation points should be selected for water movement, weather, access,
security, soil, vegetation, utilities, and building interfaces.
