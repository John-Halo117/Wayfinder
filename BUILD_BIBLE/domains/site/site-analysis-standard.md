# Site Analysis Standard

Site analysis converts observed land into reusable constraints, capabilities,
and expansion options.

## Required Analysis Layers

- legal: parcel, setbacks, easements, restrictions
- topographic: slope, grade breaks, high points, low points
- hydrologic: surface flow, ponding, erosion, infiltration, overflow
- soil: bearing, drainage, fertility, compaction, contamination risk
- climate: sun, shade, wind, frost, heat, snow, fire, storm exposure
- access: emergency, delivery, maintenance, pedestrian, livestock, equipment
- utilities: available services, ingress routes, serviceability constraints
- ecology: vegetation, habitat, invasive pressure, protected areas
- expansion: reserved corridors, future pads, blocked options

## Output

Each layer produces constraints, capabilities, observation points, and required
evidence links. The analysis does not invent a design; it defines the land's
operating envelope.
