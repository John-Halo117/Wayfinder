# Material Standards

Materials are selected for long service life, repairability, compatibility,
and known maintenance behavior.

## Longevity

Prefer materials with documented service life, known failure modes, available
replacement parts, and repair paths.

## Repairability

Prefer materials and assemblies that can be patched, refinished, replaced in
modules, or serviced without destroying adjacent systems.

## Modularity

Use dimensions, fasteners, panels, and assemblies that allow partial
replacement. Avoid monolithic assemblies where routine failure would force
broad demolition.

## Maintainability

Material records should state cleaning, coating, sealing, lubrication,
inspection, and replacement requirements.

## Environmental Resistance

Material choices must match expected exposure: water, humidity, UV, freeze,
heat, abrasion, impact, pests, soil contact, chemicals, and biological growth.

## Compatibility

Material records should state compatible adjacent materials, fasteners,
sealants, coatings, adhesives, and service environments.

## Inspection

Inspectable materials are preferred. Hidden materials must have evidence,
service life, and access or replacement strategy.

## Replacement

Replacement should preserve dimensions, interfaces, and service access unless a
decision record intentionally changes them.
