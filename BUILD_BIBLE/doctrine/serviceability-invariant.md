# Serviceability Invariant

No critical capability should require destructive access.

## Preferred Patterns

- access panels
- floor hatches
- utility chases
- mechanical corridors
- flush inspection ports
- modular assemblies
- removable panels
- labeled junctions
- documented isolation valves and disconnects

## Critical Capability

A capability is critical when its failure can compromise safety, shelter,
water, sanitation, power, climate control, security, food production, access,
or major asset protection.

## Design Test

If future service requires cutting drywall, breaking concrete, removing
permanent finishes, or guessing hidden routes, the design fails this invariant
unless a lifecycle decision explicitly accepts the tradeoff.
